# 🌱 Tiny Projects Philosophy

> Why small beats big, every single time.

---

## The Pattern Nobody Talks About

Most creators follow this loop:

```
Big idea → Excitement → Planning → More planning → Overwhelm → Abandon
```

Then they feel bad about themselves. Then they get a new big idea. Repeat.

The fix isn't more discipline. It's **smaller projects**.

---

## Why Tiny Projects Win

**They get finished.**
A finished tiny project beats an abandoned big one. Every time.

**They build momentum.**
Finishing one small thing makes it easier to start the next one.

**They compound.**
10 tiny projects = a portfolio, a body of work, proof that you ship.

**They teach you more.**
You learn more from shipping 5 small things than planning 1 big thing.

---

## The Tiny Project Rules

1. **Define "done" before you start.**
   If you don't know what finished looks like, you'll never get there.

2. **Scope it to one sitting.**
   If it can't be finished in 1-2 hours, it's too big. Cut it in half.

3. **No scope creep.**
   When you think of a cool add-on, write it down as a *future* seed. Don't add it now.

4. **Ship the ugly version.**
   Version 1 just needs to exist. It doesn't need to be good.

---

## The Low-Attention-Span Dev Version

For ML devs specifically:

- A tiny project = one notebook, one model, one insight
- A tiny post = one experiment result + one sentence of reflection
- A tiny contribution = one PR, one bug fix, one doc improvement

You don't need a research paper. You need a shipped thing.

---

## The Reframe

> You're not bad at finishing things.
> You've just been picking projects that are too big.

Start smaller than feels reasonable.
Finish it.
Feel good.
Start the next one.

That's the whole philosophy.
