# ⚡ The Low Attention Capture System

> Get the thought down before your brain moves on.

---

## The 3 Capture Speeds

### 🟢 5-Second Capture
**Use when:** A thought hits and you have zero time.

**How:** One line. No context. Just the raw spark.

```
idea: ML model that predicts when I'll abandon a project
```

Done. Move on.

---

### 🟡 10-Second Capture
**Use when:** You have a tiny bit of breathing room.

**How:** One line + one "why it matters" line.

```
idea: ML model that predicts project abandonment
why: could actually keep me accountable
```

Done. Move on.

---

### 🔵 30-Second Capture
**Use when:** The idea feels big and you don't want to lose it.

**How:** Idea + why + one possible next step.

```
idea: ML model that predicts project abandonment
why: accountability tool for low-attention devs
next: check if anyone has built this, write a seed note
```

Done. Move on.

---

## The Golden Rule

> **Capture now. Organize never (or later).**

Your job is to catch the thought. Not to file it perfectly.
A messy capture beats a perfect system you never use.

---

## Where to Capture

Pick **one** place. That's it.

- A notes app (Obsidian, Notion, Apple Notes — doesn't matter)
- A physical notebook
- A voice memo
- The daily note template in this repo

The tool doesn't matter. **Consistency does.**

---

## Turning Captures into Seeds

Once a week (or when you feel like it), scan your captures.
Anything that still feels interesting? Promote it to a **seed note**.

A seed note is just: one idea + one sentence of context.

That's the whole system.
